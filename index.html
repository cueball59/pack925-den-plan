Thu Sep 3

Meet the Den & the Scout Law

Focus: Get to know each other and hear the Scout Law for the first time.

Requirements completed tonight:

  • Bobcat 1 (Get to know den members)
  • Bobcat 2 (Hear the Scout Law; show being friendly)
Materials to bring:
  • butcher paper / poster board
  • washable paint or markers
  • wet wipes
  • printed Scout Law card
Gathering Name-Ball Toss
  1. Sit in a circle. Roll or gently toss a soft ball.
  2. Whoever catches it says their name and one thing they like (a color, an animal, a snack).
  3. Keep going until everyone has gone twice.
Opening Opening Ceremony
  1. Form up in a horseshoe or circle. Ask a Scout to lead the Pledge of Allegiance to the flag.
  2. Everyone gives the Cub Scout sign and recites the Scout Oath together.
  3. Recite the Scout Law together. Younger dens may say it line-by-line after the Den Chief.
Activity Den Name Banner
  1. Tell the Lions they are all one den - a team.
  2. Everyone adds their handprint (washable paint) or a drawing to a big banner.
  3. Hang it at every meeting so it feels like 'their' space.
Needs: butcher paper / poster board, washable paint or markers, wet wipes
Activity The Scout Law & Being Friendly
  1. Den Chief or leader reads the Scout Law out loud slowly.
  2. Zoom in on FRIENDLY. Ask: 'What does a friendly person do?'
  3. Play 'Friendly or Not?' - Den Chief acts out a scene (sharing vs grabbing); Lions thumbs-up if it's friendly.
  4. Each Lion does one friendly thing right now (give a compliment to their buddy).
Needs: printed Scout Law card
Closing Closing Ceremony - the Living Circle
  1. Form a circle. Everyone puts their left hand toward the center, little fingers linked to the next Scout.
  2. Den Chief shares a 1-sentence thought tied to tonight (e.g., 'Tonight we practiced being Helpful.').
  3. On the Den Chief's signal, everyone raises the circle and gives a quiet 'Do Your Best!' Dismiss.
Send home / follow-up:
  • Give each family the Protect Your Children booklet (Bobcat req 4).
  • Remind parents about the shared-leadership host rotation - sign up for a month.
Den Chief tip: First meeting = set the tone. Learn every kid's name tonight and use it constantly. Big energy, big smiles.

Do Your Best!

Focus: Learn the Cub Scout motto and share a time you did your best.

Requirements completed tonight:

  • Bobcat 3 (Share a 'Do Your Best' moment)
Materials to bring:
  • beanbags
  • bucket
  • painter's tape for a balance line
  • chair
Gathering Freeze Dance
  1. Play music; Lions dance. Stop the music - everyone freezes.
  2. Anyone still moving does a silly pose and rejoins. Pure wiggle-burner.

  1. Teach the motto: 'Do Your Best.' Say it together 3 times, louder each time.
  2. Den Chief shares a quick example (tried hard on something even when it was tricky).
  3. Go around the circle: each Lion tells (or acts out) one time they did their best. Adult partners can help them remember.
Activity Best-Effort Obstacle Mini-Course
  1. Set up 4 simple stations (hop 5 times, crawl under a chair, toss a beanbag in a bucket, balance on a line).
  2. Each Lion goes through cheering 'Do Your Best!'
  3. Everyone finishes - it's about effort, not winning.
Needs: beanbags, bucket, painter's tape for a balance line, chair

Six Essentials & Staying Safe

Focus: Learn the Six Essentials and the S.A.W. rule before we head outside next time.

Requirements completed tonight:

  • Mountain Lion 1 (Six Essentials)
  • Mountain Lion 3 (What S.A.W. means)
Materials to bring:
  • a full set of the Six Essentials to pass around
  • a whistle for each Lion (or share a few)
Gathering Pack the Pack Relay
  1. Pile of gear on one side (real + silly items). Backpack on the other.
  2. Lions relay-run to grab ONLY the real Six Essentials and pack them.
Opening Opening Ceremony
  1. Form up in a horseshoe or circle. Ask a Scout to lead the Pledge of Allegiance to the flag.
  2. Everyone gives the Cub Scout sign and recites the Scout Oath together.
  3. Recite the Scout Law together. Younger dens may say it line-by-line after the Den Chief.
Activity The Six Essentials Show-and-Tell
  1. Hold up each of the Six Essentials one at a time. Say what it's for.
  2. Six Essentials: water, flashlight, first-aid kit, trail snack, sun protection, whistle.
  3. Each Lion picks one item and shows the den what you DO with it.
Needs: a full set of the Six Essentials to pass around
Activity S.A.W. - If You Get Separated
  1. Teach S.A.W.: STOP where you are, ALERT by blowing your whistle 3 times, WAIT for a trusted adult.
  2. Practice: Den Chief yells 'You're separated!' - Lions freeze, blow whistle 3 times, sit and wait.
  3. Repeat twice so it sticks.
Needs: a whistle for each Lion (or share a few)

Send home / follow-up:

Nature Walk: Natural vs Manmade

Focus: Take a 20-minute walk with our Six Essentials and spot animals.

Requirements completed tonight:

  • Mountain Lion 2 (20-min outdoor walk; natural vs manmade)
  • Mountain Lion 4 (Common animals: wild vs domestic)
Materials to bring:
  • the Six Essentials
  • a nearby safe walking area
  • a few animal picture cards (optional)
Gathering Buddy Up
  1. Each Lion + adult partner is a buddy pair. Practice 'buddy check!' - everyone finds their buddy fast.
Opening Opening Ceremony
  1. Form up in a horseshoe or circle. Ask a Scout to lead the Pledge of Allegiance to the flag.
  2. Everyone gives the Cub Scout sign and recites the Scout Oath together.
  3. Recite the Scout Law together. Younger dens may say it line-by-line after the Den Chief.

  1. Walk a safe loop outside for about 20 minutes with the Six Essentials.
  2. As you go, call out: 'Natural or manmade?' Point at a tree (natural), a sign (manmade), a rock, a fence.
  3. Have Lions collect ZERO items - we look, we don't take (Leave No Trace, kid version).
Needs: the Six Essentials, a nearby safe walking area
Activity Animal Sort
  1. During/after the walk, name animals you might see here.
  2. Sort them: WILD (squirrel, hawk, deer) vs DOMESTICATED (dog, cat, cow).
  3. Each Lion names one wild and one tame animal.

  1. Put a little washable glitter ('germs') on Lions' hands.

Den Chief Playbook

You're the Den Chief - a Scouts BSA youth leader helping run a Cub Scout den. You are the coolest person in the room to these kids; they will copy everything you do. Your job is to help the adult den leader run a fun, on-time meeting and to be a role model.

Before the meeting

  • Show up 10-15 minutes early in your field uniform (Class A).
  • Ask the den leader what the plan is and which parts they want YOU to run.
  • Set out materials and get the gathering game ready before Scouts arrive.

During the meeting

  • Run the gathering game and lead the ceremonies (Oath/Law) with energy.
  • Demonstrate activities using EDGE: Explain, Demonstrate, Guide, Enable.
  • Keep the pace up. If an activity is done, move on. Bored Cubs get wiggly.
  • Use praise, not scolding. Catch kids doing the right thing and call it out.
  • Never be alone one-on-one with a Cub Scout (Youth Protection - stay in sight of others).

After the meeting

  • Get the Cubs to help clean up - make it a race.
  • Ask the leader how it went and what's next week.
  • Log your service hours - this counts toward your rank and the Den Chief Service Award.

Your toolkit

  • A few extra pencils and sharpies
  • A whistle (one blast = freeze and look at me)
  • 2-3 backup games in your pocket for when you finish early
  • A positive attitude - your energy sets the room

The 6-part meeting flow (every week)

  1. Before (10 min): Den Chief + adult leader set up. Lay out materials. Write tonight's plan on a board or paper so everyone can see it.
  2. Gathering (10 min): As Scouts arrive they jump straight into the gathering game so no one is standing around. The host adult/Den Chief runs it while the leader takes attendance and collects any dues.
  3. Opening (5 min): Everyone stands. Do the flag/opening ceremony: Pledge of Allegiance, Scout Oath, and Scout Law. Keep it crisp.
  4. Activities (25-30 min): The main event - the adventure activities for tonight. This is where the requirements get done.
  5. Closing (5 min): Leader gives reminders (next meeting date, what to bring). Den Chief leads a short closing thought and the Living Circle.
  6. After (5 min): Scouts help put the room back in order. Leader + Den Chief quickly preview next week.

Say-it-from-memory

Scout Oath: On my honor I will do my best to do my duty to God and my country and to obey the Scout Law; to help other people at all times; to keep myself physically strong, mentally awake, and morally straight.

Scout Law: A Scout is Trustworthy, Loyal, Helpful, Friendly, Courteous, Kind, Obedient, Cheerful, Thrifty, Brave, Clean, and Reverent.

Cub Scout Motto: Do Your Best.

Cub Scout Six Essentials: 1) Water bottle, 2) Flashlight, 3) First-aid kit, 4) Trail food/snack, 5) Sun protection, 6) Whistle. (A jacket/rain gear is a smart 'plus one.')
