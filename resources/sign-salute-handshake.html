The Cub Scout Sign

Two fingers make a "V" — they stand for the two parts of the Promise and look like a wolf's pointed ears, alert and ready.

How: Raise your right hand, palm forward. Point your index and middle finger straight up. Your thumb holds down your ring and pinky fingers.
When: Give the sign when you say the Scout Oath and Scout Law, and whenever a leader raises the sign — that means "Signs up, quiet down," so stop talking and raise yours too.

The Cub Scout Salute

The salute is a way to show respect — to the flag, to leaders, and to fellow Scouts.

How: Make the Cub Scout sign with your right hand, then touch the two fingertips to your forehead, just above your right eyebrow (or the brim of your cap). Keep your arm and hand straight, then snap it down.
When: Salute during the flag ceremony and the Pledge of Allegiance, or to greet a leader in uniform.

The Cub Scout Handshake

Scouts shake with the left hand — the hand closest to your heart. It is a special sign of friendship and trust.

How: Shake using your left hand. Hold your first two fingers along the other person's wrist, look them in the eye, and give a friendly shake.
When: Use the handshake to greet other Scouts and leaders, welcome a new friend to the den, or say thank you.
